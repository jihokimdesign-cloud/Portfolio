Context / Problem

Users Were Dropping Off — But Why?

TAP3D is a B2B VR training platform preparing workers for advanced manufacturing roles, offering a seamless experience between VR and Web.

Despite a seamless platform, internal data revealed a significant lag in the training completion cycle. Usage logs showed a clear pattern — learners were dropping off early, unable to keep pace with initial modules.

Research

9 Interviews. 3 Barriers.

Conducted 9 user interviews and performed affinity mapping on 200+ coded quotes. Research centered on: “What triggers a user to start right now?” and “What is the minimum information needed to commit?”

Research confirmed three critical cognitive barriers that explained the drop-off:

✗Barrier
Commitment Debt
"Feels too long." Learners deferred because a "full lecture" felt like a heavy burden.
✓Solution
Bite-sized Checklist
AI recommends 5–10 min actions instead of full lectures, making starting feel effortless.
✗Barrier
Decision Paralysis
"I have so much to do, but I don't know what to do first."
✓Solution
Personalized Suggestion
AI picks the best next step with a one-line rationale — "Here's a 5-min safety check. Do this now."
✗Barrier
Support Gap
"No quick help." Without an immediate way to get answers, learners would defer tasks to "tomorrow."
✓Solution
Context-Aware Handoff
Users upload a recording → AI auto-drafts a message with context for the instructor. A "rage quit" becomes a seamless escalation.
The pattern was clear: users weren't rejecting the product — they were overwhelmed by the onboarding experience. The solution needed to reduce cognitive load, not add more features.

Reflection

What I'd Carry Forward

This project taught me how to navigate constraints and make strategic trade-offs. Proving value through simulation before building was the key insight — it reduced risk for the business while giving users evidence-backed improvements.

  • Wizard of Oz testing is powerful for validating AI concepts cheaply
  • Data-driven storytelling wins stakeholder buy-in faster than mockups alone
  • Ship the simplest thing first, then prove the bigger vision
Pre-launch simulation testing seeded a culture of data-driven design at TAP3D — transforming how the team approaches new features.
